Rising fuel prices result in consumers being less likely to purchase larger, more expensive vehicles, such as sports utility vehicles or luxury automobiles, and more likely to purchase smaller, less expensive and more fuel-efficient vehicles. The proposed rule rolls back future model year fuel economy targets, reduces annual increases and removes the consideration of the availability of alternative fuel technology, including EVs, from the fuel economy targets, though the substance and timing of the final rule is uncertain. However, any future standards that result in stricter fuel economy standards could significantly increase our costs of operation as well as reduce our volume of business.

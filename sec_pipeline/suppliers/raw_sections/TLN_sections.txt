Commercial transactions with future delivery dates may meet certain accounting criteria requiring them to be carried on the balance sheet at fair value. The “mark-to-market” effect, or remeasurement of these transactions to fair value at current market prices, is generally recognized in earnings through contract delivery. However, many commercial transactions with future delivery dates do not meet the criteria for “mark-to-market” accounting, and the income effect of these transactions is generally recognized at contract delivery.
